Market Research

Now that you know what problem you want to start to solve, you can do some research to find out if this idea would work on the market.

If you are just building a project for yourself, your market research is yourself. So you can skip this step, but you can also try to do this exercise with yourself to make sure you are building a solution that will solve the problem in a way that will work for you. The goal is to use the software once you build it.

Come up with a list of questions you could ask to figure out if the problem you are solving is worth solving, and how it should best be solved.

  1. Is the problem worth solving?
  1. What solutions to the problem are most desired?

This is how you will begin to build project requirements. Your narrowed problem statement is the guardrail, and you need to fill in requirements that stay within that guardrail.

Example

What problem are you trying to solve?

I aim to address the challenges faced by elderly individuals who may encounter difficulties in performing daily tasks, such as grocery shopping and other errands. As people age, physical limitations, health issues, or mobility constraints can make it challenging for them to independently manage these essential activities.

Survey Questions:

Daily Routine:
  • What are some daily tasks that you find challenging or difficult to do on your own?
  • Can you walk me through a typical day for you and highlight any tasks that require assistance?
Mobility and Transportation:
  • Do you face any challenges getting around or going to places you need to visit?
  • Is transportation a concern for you when it comes to accessing essential services?
Health and Medication:
  • How do you manage your medications, and are there any difficulties you encounter in this process?
  • Are there specific health-related tasks or information you wish you could easily access through technology?
Communication:
  • How do you stay in touch with family and friends? Are there any challenges in using communication tools?
  • Is there anything specific you would like to share with your loved ones but find difficult to express?
Shopping and Errands:
  • Do you face any difficulties with grocery shopping or running errands?
  • What would make it easier for you to ensure you have the things you need at home?
Home Safety:
  • Are there any safety concerns you have within your home? What improvements would make you feel safer?
  • How do you handle emergencies or unexpected situations at home?
Social Connection:
  • How important is socializing for you, and are there any barriers to staying socially active?
  • Is there anything you would like to do or attend in the community, but find challenging to participate in?
Technology Usage:
  • Are you comfortable using technology like smartphones or tablets? What aspects do you find easy or challenging?
  • Are there specific tasks or information you believe technology could help you with?
Nutrition and Meals:
  • How do you plan and prepare meals? Are there any challenges in maintaining a balanced diet?
  • Are there specific dietary preferences or restrictions you would like assistance with?
Quality of Life:
  • What would significantly improve your overall quality of life on a day-to-day basis?
  • Is there anything you wish you could do independently that you currently find challenging?
